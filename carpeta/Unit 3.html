Unit 3 - Interactive Communication

Unit Objectives:

  • Develop comprehensive communication skills in English, including speaking, listening, and cultural understanding, through interactive activities and engaging discussions.
  • Foster confidence and fluency in real-life conversational contexts by practicing interactive conversations, listening comprehension, and exploring diverse cultural perspectives.
  • Enhance language proficiency and intercultural communication competencies to effectively engage with English speakers from different backgrounds and cultures.

Class 7: Interactive Conversations

Objectives: Develop conversational skills by engaging in interactive dialogues. Practice asking and answering questions in various social contexts. Improve fluency and confidence in speaking English through interactive sessions.

Class 8: Developing Listening and Comprehension Skills

Objectives: Enhance listening comprehension abilities in English. Practice identifying main ideas, details, and implied meanings in spoken conversations. Develop strategies for effective listening, such as note-taking and active listening techniques.

Class 9: Exploring Culture and Diversity Through English

Objectives: Gain awareness and understanding of cultural diversity through English language activities. Explore cultural norms, customs, and traditions from different English-speaking regions. Foster cultural sensitivity and empathy by engaging with diverse perspectives and experiences.

These units will be conducted synchronously through Zoom.

Class 10: Final Review and Unit Evaluation

Objectives: Review key concepts, vocabulary, and skills covered in Unit 3. Assess student progress and learning outcomes through a comprehensive unit evaluation. Provide feedback and reflection opportunities to enhance future language learning strategies.

English Proficiency Final Exam

Oral Exam

  1. Presentation

    Introduce yourself including information about your name, age, gender, nationality, occupation, and favorite color. Use the verb "to be" correctly.

  2. Question and Answer

    Take turns asking and answering questions about the information presented in the introductions. Use the verb "to be" and vocabulary related to the exam objectives.

Written Exam

  1. Fill in the Blanks

    Complete the sentences with the correct form of the verb "to be" (am, is, are).

  2. Vocabulary Matching

    Match the vocabulary words with their corresponding definitions.

  3. Descriptive Writing

    Write a paragraph describing a person including details about their age, gender, nationality, occupation, and favorite color.

  4. Conversation Dialogue

    Write a dialogue between two people meeting for the first time. Include introductions with information about name, age, nationality, occupation, and favorite color. Use the verb "to be" correctly.
